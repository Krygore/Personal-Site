About me

The man behind the code...

My Background

Born and raised in Washington State USA, graduated from high school in 2014 and dove head first into a career in physical labor working landscaping and excavation! After 7 years, my body couldn't take the abuse anymore, so I decided to expand my horizons and look towards technology...

I began learning front end web development in February of 2021 through freecodecamp.org, where I took a beginner level course in Responsive Web Design. From there, I used various learning platforms and resources such as W3schools.com, MDN Web Docs, Udemy.com and more to further my education and start learning back-end development.

Outside of technology, I also enjoy hobbies such as freshwater fish keeping and aquascaping as well as chainsaw carving.

My Hobbies

Fish Keeping

Fish keeping is something I got into about 2 years ago when I was in a pet store looking for a new dog food. I saw a really cool looking betta fish and decided I wanted to get it. I got a little 3.5 gallon aquarium kit, set it up, had the fish in it for a while and immediately had other ideas. A couple years later, I've got 3 tanks with over 50 fish, all tropical fish.

Aquascaping is a huge part of fish keeping. You get to design the layout and theme of the tank and, if you choose live plants like I have, you can literally watch it grow and flourish.

Some of the fish I have include, but are not limited to:

  • Betta Fish
  • Psuedomugal Rainbow Fish
  • Gouramis (Variety)
  • Tetras (Variety)
  • Barbs
  • Loaches (Variety)

For more information about my hobby in fish keeping, check out this page.

Chainsaw Carving

I've always had a creative side. One day I was out cutting fire wood when I ran out of room in the truck and still had a couple logs left to cut. I picked one up, set it on top of another, and started cutting angles into the wood until a shape started to appear. After about 30 minutes of playing around, I had myself a little carving that was about 14 inches tall and 12 inches round. It wasn't perfect, but it was good enough to be recognized as a mushroom.

I became enthralled with the craft and watched hundreds of hours of youtube videos and looking at pictures of carvings on google and pintrest.

Since then, I have carved owls, wolves, dragons, fish, and even made a couple benches. In some cases, people have even asked me to carve them certain items for a commission!

To see some of my carvings and even some videos of me carving, check out this page.
